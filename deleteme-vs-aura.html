DeleteMe vs Aura

A head-to-head comparison for 2026

The bottom line

DeleteMe is a focused data removal service with recurring automated opt-outs. Aura is a full identity protection suite that includes data removal as one piece of a larger package. If all you need is data removal, DeleteMe does more of it. If you want identity theft monitoring, phishing protection, and spam blocking bundled together, Aura covers more ground. Neither service is perfect. DeleteMe scored just 27% in the Consumer Reports removal study. Aura skips major brokers like Spokeo and BeenVerified. Your choice depends on whether you want depth or breadth.

DeleteMe

The original data removal service

From $8.71/mo

DeleteMe is one of the oldest and most recognized names in data removal. It focuses specifically on finding your personal information on people-search sites and submitting opt-out requests on your behalf. The service runs on a recurring basis, so it keeps checking for new listings after the initial removal.

Setup is straightforward. You enter your personal details and DeleteMe handles the rest. You get progress reports showing which brokers have been contacted and which removals are complete. The dashboard is clean and easy to follow.

That said, performance has been questioned. The Consumer Reports study found that DeleteMe removed only 27% of user profiles after four months of testing. That was the lowest result among the seven services tested. Some users also report being limited to 10 requests per quarter, which can feel restrictive if your data appears on many sites.

Aura

Full identity protection suite with data removal

From $12/mo

Aura is not just a data removal tool. It is a full identity protection platform that bundles data removal with spam blocking, phishing protection, identity theft monitoring, and more. If you want a single subscription that covers multiple privacy threats, Aura is designed for that.

The interface is polished and one of the best we have seen. Aura covers roughly 140 to 200 data broker sites and includes a family plan, money-back guarantee, and 24/7 support. The broader feature set makes it appealing if you are worried about more than just people-search sites.

However, there are real gaps in its data removal capabilities. Aura does not cover major brokers like Spokeo, BeenVerified, or Intelius. It does not offer recurring automated removals or manual opt-out controls. And several users have reported unexpected renewal price hikes with no advance notice. One long-term subscriber saw his annual fee jump from $120 to $199. Available in the USA only.

Detailed comparison

Pricing

DeleteMe starts at $8.71/mo when billed annually. Aura starts at $12/mo. On paper, DeleteMe is about 27% cheaper. But the two services are not directly comparable because Aura bundles identity theft monitoring, spam blocking, and phishing protection into its price. If you would pay for those features separately, Aura could actually save you money.

One important difference is the money-back guarantee. Aura offers one. DeleteMe does not. If you are not sure which service to try first, Aura is the lower-risk option from a financial standpoint.

Watch out for Aura's renewal pricing. Multiple users have reported significant price increases at renewal time with no prior notice. Make sure to check your renewal terms before committing to an annual plan.

Broker coverage

DeleteMe focuses on people-search sites and runs recurring opt-out requests on your behalf. It covers the most commonly cited data brokers and processes removals on an ongoing basis. However, you are limited to 10 requests per quarter.

Aura covers 140 to 200 broker sites. That number sounds reasonable, but there are notable gaps. Aura does not cover Spokeo, BeenVerified, or Intelius. Those are three of the largest and most frequently cited people-search platforms. If your information appears on those sites, Aura will not help you remove it.

Aura also does not offer recurring automated removals. Once it sends opt-out requests, there is no ongoing monitoring to catch re-listings. DeleteMe handles that automatically. For pure data removal depth, DeleteMe has the edge.

Real user experience

Both services get mixed reviews. DeleteMe users praise the simple setup and brand trust, but several report that removals are incomplete. The Consumer Reports study confirmed this with a 27% removal rate after four months.

Aura users appreciate the polished interface and the broader security features. Some report that Aura caught brokers that DeleteMe missed. But others say there is no way to verify whether removals actually happened. The lack of transparency around removal proof is a recurring complaint.

Both services offer 24/7 support, which is a plus compared to some competitors that only offer business-hours help.

Who should pick which

Pick DeleteMe if you want a dedicated data removal service with recurring opt-outs. It is cheaper, covers more of the major people-search sites, and handles re-listings automatically. It works best for people who want a set-it-and-forget-it approach to removing their data from broker sites. It also works in Europe, which Aura does not.

Pick Aura if you want broader identity protection beyond just data removal. If phishing alerts, spam blocking, identity theft monitoring, and a polished dashboard matter to you, Aura packages all of that into one subscription. It is a better fit for people who want a single tool for multiple privacy and security concerns. The money-back guarantee also makes it easier to test.

Consider using both if your privacy needs are serious. One Reddit user reported running Aura alongside DeleteMe specifically because Aura caught brokers that DeleteMe missed. The two services complement each other rather than fully overlapping.

Final verdict

DeleteMe is the better pure data removal service. It offers recurring automated removals, covers major brokers that Aura skips, and costs less per month. But its 27% removal rate in the Consumer Reports study is hard to ignore.

Aura is the better all-in-one security platform. You get data removal plus identity theft monitoring, phishing protection, and spam blocking in a single subscription. But its data removal capabilities have real gaps, and the lack of recurring removals means your information can reappear without you knowing.

If data removal is your primary goal, go with DeleteMe. If you want a broader security package that includes some data removal, Aura is worth considering. And if privacy is a serious concern, running both together gives you the widest coverage.

Frequently asked questions

Is Aura better than DeleteMe for data removal?

Not for pure data removal. DeleteMe offers recurring automated removals and covers major brokers like Spokeo and BeenVerified that Aura skips. However, Aura bundles data removal with identity theft monitoring, spam blocking, and phishing protection. If you want an all-in-one security tool, Aura offers more features overall.

Does Aura actually remove your data from broker sites?

Aura sends opt-out requests to 140 to 200 broker sites. Some users confirm it catches brokers that other services miss. However, it does not cover Spokeo, BeenVerified, or Intelius, and it does not offer recurring removals. Several users also report that there is no way to verify whether removals actually went through.

Can I use DeleteMe and Aura together?

Yes. Some users run both services at the same time. Aura can catch broker sites that DeleteMe does not cover, and DeleteMe provides the recurring automated removals that Aura lacks. Using both gives you wider coverage, though it also means paying for two subscriptions.

Why did Aura raise its renewal price without warning?

Multiple users on Trustpilot and Reddit have reported unexpected price increases at renewal. One user saw his annual fee jump from $120 to $199. Aura has not publicly addressed this pattern. If you sign up, check your renewal terms carefully and set a calendar reminder before your plan renews.
