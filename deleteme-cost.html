How Much Does DeleteMe Cost?

Full pricing breakdown for 2026, with cheaper alternatives

How much does DeleteMe cost?

DeleteMe costs $10.75 per month for 1 person, billed annually at $129 per year. There is no monthly billing option. The 2-year plan drops to $8.71/mo ($209 total). Couples pay $229/year and families of 4 pay $329/year. All plans include the same features. There is no free tier and no money-back guarantee.

DeleteMe only offers annual and 2-year billing. There is no month-to-month option. All plans include the same core features: automated data broker opt-outs, quarterly privacy reports, and 24/7 customer support. The only differences are subscription length and the number of people covered.

DeleteMe Premium plan

DeleteMe also offers a Premium plan at $180/year for 1 person. Premium adds bi-monthly reports (instead of quarterly), 60 custom removal requests per year (instead of 40), and coverage of 15 additional data broker sites beyond the standard list.

DeleteMe business pricing

DeleteMe offers business and enterprise plans for organizations. These require a custom quote based on the number of employees. Business plans are not listed on the public pricing page.

DeleteMe discounts

Military personnel and veterans can get up to 20% off through ID.me verification. DeleteMe occasionally runs promotional discounts, but there is no student discount or referral program.

What does DeleteMe include?

Every DeleteMe plan includes the same features regardless of price tier:

  • Automated opt-out requests submitted to data broker sites on your behalf
  • Quarterly privacy reports showing which brokers were contacted and removal status
  • Recurring monitoring to catch data that reappears after removal
  • 24/7 customer support via email
  • Coverage in the USA and Europe

DeleteMe does not include manual removal controls, a mobile app, or a money-back guarantee. You are limited to 40 custom removal requests per year on the standard plan (60 on Premium).

Is DeleteMe worth the cost?

At $129 per year, DeleteMe is a mid-range data removal service. It has strong brand recognition and a simple onboarding process. But the data raises questions about value for money.

The Consumer Reports data removal study found that DeleteMe removed only 27% of profiles after four months of testing. That was the lowest among all seven services tested.

Meanwhile, manual opt-out requests submitted by users themselves achieved a 70% removal rate in the same study. That means doing it yourself was nearly three times more effective than paying DeleteMe to do it.

Users on Reddit and Trustpilot report mixed results. Some long-term subscribers are satisfied, but others mention missed listings and the 10-request-per-quarter limit on the standard plan. The lack of a money-back guarantee means there is no risk-free way to test the service.
