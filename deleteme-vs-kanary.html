A detailed comparison based on real tests, user reviews, and third-party data

Is Kanary or DeleteMe better?

It depends on what you prioritize. Kanary has the largest broker list of any data removal service, offers manual removal controls, and lets you try it free before paying. DeleteMe is 42% cheaper at $8.71/mo, includes 24/7 support, and works in both the USA and Europe. Kanary is the better choice for thorough broker coverage. DeleteMe is cheaper and simpler. Kanary goes deeper. If you want a set-it-and-forget-it service at a lower price with 24/7 support and coverage in Europe, go with DeleteMe. DeleteMe

The original data removal service

From $8.71/mo

DeleteMe launched in 2011 and remains the most recognized name in data removal. You sign up, enter your personal details, and the service handles opt-out requests across dozens of people-search sites on a recurring basis.

The onboarding is fast. The dashboard is clean. You get regular progress reports showing which brokers have been contacted and which listings have been removed. DeleteMe is available in both the USA and Europe, which gives it a geographic edge over many competitors including Kanary. If you are comparing other options, see our DeleteMe vs Incogni breakdown for another popular alternative.

The weakness is performance. In the Consumer Reports data removal study, DeleteMe achieved only a 27% removal rate after four months. That was the lowest score among all seven services tested. You also get no manual removal controls and are limited to 10 removal requests per quarter.

Signing up takes about five minutes. You enter your name, email, date of birth, and any addresses or phone numbers you want scrubbed. DeleteMe's team takes it from there. You will receive your first privacy report within about seven days, and recurring scans happen automatically after that. It is one of the simplest onboarding flows in the industry, which is part of why it remains so popular.

Kanary

Deep coverage with hands-on control

From $14.99/mo

Kanary takes a different approach. It has the largest broker list of any data removal service we have tested, and its exposure detection is the most accurate. You get both automated recurring removals and manual opt-out controls, which is rare in this space.

The first progress report arrives within 48 hours. That is much faster than the roughly 7-day wait with DeleteMe. Kanary also offers a free trial so you can test the service before committing, plus a money-back guarantee if you are not satisfied. For a comparison with another service that emphasizes deep broker coverage, see our DeleteMe vs Optery page.

The downside is speed. TechRadar's hands-on testing found that after two weeks, Kanary had only completed six removals. Brokers have up to 45 days to comply with opt-out requests, so full results take 30 to 90 days. Kanary also lacks 24/7 support and is available in the USA only.

The signup process takes about ten minutes. Kanary asks for more detail upfront than DeleteMe does, including aliases and old addresses, which feeds its broader exposure scan. Once you finish, the dashboard shows every broker where your data was found and lets you track each removal individually. That level of transparency is something most competitors do not offer.

Pricing comparison

DeleteMe starts at $8.71 per month when billed annually, which comes to about $104.52 per year. Kanary starts at $14.99 per month. That makes DeleteMe about 42% cheaper on a monthly basis.

On an annual basis, the gap adds up. You save roughly $75 per year by choosing DeleteMe over Kanary. That is a meaningful difference, especially if you plan to keep the service running long-term, which most people should since data brokers re-list your information regularly.

However, Kanary offers a free trial that lets you see your exposure report before paying anything. It also includes a money-back guarantee. DeleteMe offers neither. If you are uncertain about committing, Kanary gives you more flexibility to test the waters.

Both services offer family plans. DeleteMe's family plan covers two people for $15.12 per month billed annually. Kanary's family plan is priced higher, but it includes the same deep broker coverage and manual controls for each family member.

On a per-broker basis, Kanary may actually deliver better value despite the higher price. Since Kanary covers more brokers than DeleteMe and has no cap on removal requests, you are paying for broader protection. DeleteMe's lower price comes with a smaller broker list and a 10-request-per-quarter limit, which reduces the effective value if your data appears on many sites.

If budget is your primary concern, DeleteMe wins on price. If you want to try before you buy, Kanary's free trial is a significant advantage. For a budget-focused alternative to both, see our DeleteMe vs Incogni comparison where Incogni starts at just $7.99 per month.

Broker coverage

Kanary has the largest broker list we have seen. It covers more people-search sites and data brokers than DeleteMe, and its exposure detection is consistently more accurate in identifying where your data appears.

DeleteMe covers the most well-known and dangerous data brokers, but its list is smaller. It also limits you to 10 removal requests per quarter, which can be frustrating if your data appears on many sites. That cap means DeleteMe may not address all your exposures within a single billing cycle. If you have data on 30 or 40 brokers, it could take multiple quarters to clear them all.

Kanary has no such limit. It submits removal requests to every broker where it finds your data, with no quarterly cap. If breadth of coverage matters to you, Kanary is the clear winner here.

In terms of broker categories, both services handle the big people-search sites like Spokeo, BeenVerified, and WhitePages. Where Kanary pulls ahead is in covering smaller, more obscure data aggregators and marketing list brokers that many services skip entirely. These secondary brokers are often the ones that feed data back to the larger sites, so removing your information from them helps prevent re-listing.

If you want deep broker coverage without Kanary's price tag, Optery is another option that scored well in the Consumer Reports study and offers tiered plans with varying levels of broker access.

How each service works

How DeleteMe works
  1. Sign up and enter your details. You provide your name, email, date of birth, phone numbers, and current and past addresses. The process takes about five minutes.
  2. DeleteMe scans data brokers. Their team searches dozens of people-search sites for listings containing your information.
  3. Opt-out requests are submitted. DeleteMe submits removal requests on your behalf to each broker where your data appears.
  4. You receive a privacy report. Within about seven days, you get your first report showing which brokers were contacted and which removals are in progress.
  5. Recurring scans continue. DeleteMe re-scans on a regular schedule and submits new removal requests as brokers re-list your data.
How Kanary works
  1. Sign up and provide detailed info. Kanary asks for more detail than DeleteMe, including aliases and old addresses. This takes about ten minutes.
  2. Kanary runs an exposure scan. The scan checks the largest broker list in the industry and identifies every site where your data appears.
  3. Automated removal requests go out. Kanary submits opt-out requests to every identified broker with no quarterly cap.
  4. You get a progress report within 48 hours. The dashboard shows each broker, its removal status, and lets you track progress in real time.
  5. Manual controls are available. If a broker is not responding, you can use Kanary's manual tools to submit your own opt-out request directly.
  6. Full removals complete in 30 to 90 days. Brokers have up to 45 days to comply, so patience is required for full results.

Company backgrounds

DeleteMe was founded in 2011 by Abine, a privacy-focused company based in the United States. It was one of the first consumer data removal services on the market and has built up over 15 years of operating history. That track record gives it a credibility advantage, especially with journalists, legal professionals, and security-conscious users who have relied on it for years. DeleteMe has processed millions of removal requests and is one of the most widely cited services in privacy discussions.

Kanary was founded around 2021 and is a newer entrant to the data removal space. Despite its shorter history, Kanary has differentiated itself by building the largest broker list in the industry and offering manual removal controls that most competitors lack. The company has focused on technical depth and transparency, giving users more visibility into where their data appears and how removals are progressing.

Both companies are legitimate and operate in the United States. If longevity and brand recognition matter to you, DeleteMe has the edge. If you prioritize technical capability and the broadest coverage, Kanary has invested heavily in those areas despite being newer.

Privacy and security

Both DeleteMe and Kanary require sensitive personal information to function. You are handing over your name, date of birth, phone numbers, email addresses, and physical addresses so that these services can find and remove your data from broker sites. That means trusting the company with exactly the kind of information you are trying to protect.

DeleteMe stores your data on encrypted servers and states that it does not sell or share your personal information. The company has been handling this data since 2011, and there are no known data breaches associated with the service. DeleteMe deletes your personal information from its own systems if you cancel your subscription, though the timeline for that removal is not always clear.

Kanary similarly encrypts personal data and states that it is only used for the purpose of data removal. Kanary's newer infrastructure means it was built with more modern security practices from the start. On cancellation, Kanary states that your data is removed from their systems, though you should confirm the specific retention policy before signing up.

Neither service has been independently audited for security practices in the way that Incogni has been audited by Deloitte. If third-party security verification is important to you, that is worth considering. For most users, both DeleteMe and Kanary handle data responsibly, but you should review each company's privacy policy before sharing your details.

Real user experience

DeleteMe has a larger review base. It holds roughly a 4.0 out of 5 on Trustpilot with over a thousand reviews. Many users praise the simple setup and reliable recurring removals. Others complain about missed listings and the 10-request-per-quarter limit.

Kanary has fewer reviews overall. Users who like it praise the deep broker coverage and manual controls. The most common complaint is speed. One user reported it took 3 to 6 months to remove 80% of their information. Another said the last 20% was never removed at all.

The Consumer Reports study tested DeleteMe but not Kanary. DeleteMe scored a 27% removal rate after four months, which was the lowest result among all seven services in the study. That is a significant data point to consider.

TechRadar tested Kanary directly and found only 6 completed removals after two weeks. That is partly because brokers can take up to 45 days to process opt-out requests. But it means you should not expect instant results from either service.

On Reddit, the sentiment around DeleteMe is mixed. Long-time users often defend the service as reliable over years of use, but newer subscribers frequently report disappointment with the number of brokers that are missed. The 10-request-per-quarter limit is one of the most cited frustrations in r/privacy threads.

Kanary sentiment on Reddit is more positive among privacy enthusiasts who value manual controls and transparency. The most common criticism is the price. Several users in r/privacy threads have noted that at nearly $15 per month, Kanary needs to deliver noticeably better results than cheaper alternatives to justify the cost. Users who ran both services simultaneously reported that Kanary found brokers that DeleteMe missed, but DeleteMe occasionally caught listings that Kanary's scan did not flag.

Final verdict

There is no single winner here. DeleteMe and Kanary serve different types of users.

DeleteMe is the better choice if you want a simple, affordable service that works across the USA and Europe. It has 24/7 support and a clean interface. But the 27% removal rate in the Consumer Reports study is hard to ignore, and the 10-request-per-quarter cap limits what it can do for people with widespread data exposure.

Kanary is the better choice if thoroughness matters more than speed or price. It scans more brokers, gives you manual control, and lets you try the service for free. But it costs more, takes longer to complete removals, lacks 24/7 support, and only works in the USA.

If you are still unsure, start with Kanary's free trial to see how exposed your data actually is. You can always switch to DeleteMe later if you prefer a simpler experience. And if neither feels like the right fit, our homepage covers all the top data removal alternatives for 2026.
